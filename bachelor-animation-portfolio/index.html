    Portfolio Evaluation Rubric

    Your submitted portfolio will be evaluated based on the following criteria. Each category is worth up to 15 points, with passing scores starting at 10.5 points. Please review this rubric to understand how your work will be assessed.

    Scoring Categories

    Each portfolio item will be scored according to the following scale:

    • Incomplete (0): No work submitted or content is unrelated to the assignment.
    • Unacceptable (1-5): Minimal demonstration of required skills.
    • Developing (6-10): Basic skills demonstrated but needs significant improvement.
    • Acceptable/PASS (10.5): Meets basic requirements with competent execution.
    • Emerging (11-12): Shows promise beyond basic requirements.
    • Accomplished (13-14): Demonstrates strong skills and artistic vision.
    • Exceptional (15): Exemplifies outstanding skill, creativity, and artistic excellence.

    Evaluation Categories

    Your portfolio will be evaluated on the following elements:

    • Life Drawings - Long Form Studies: Anatomical accuracy, form, structure, and balanced poses.
    • Life Drawings - Gesture Studies: Line quality, action, balance, and performance in poses.
    • Hand Studies: Anatomical accuracy, gesture, line quality, and pose dynamics.
    • Sketchbook Drawings: Composition, narrative elements, and storytelling ability.
    • Character Design/Rotation: Consistency in design and proportions across all required views.
    • Environment Drawing: Understanding of two-point perspective, composition, and character integration.
    • Storyboard: Camera angles, staging, composition, and narrative flow.
    • Personal Artwork: Artistic techniques, personal expression, and creative vision.
